Sample Lesson Plan, Assignments, and Activities


During my internship at the University of Mary Washington, I had the opportunity to lead a class session on restriction enzymes. Downloadable versions of the lesson outline, lecture slides, and activity from this class session are linked below. It should be noted that I drafted the lesson outline and lecture slides but did not create the Paper Restriction Enzyme Activity. This activity was created by Swati Agrawal (University of Mary Washington), Matthew Mastropaolo (Neumann University), Sara Anderson (Minnesota State University Moorhead), Bryan Gibb (New York Institute of Technology), and Dustin Edwards (Tarleton State University). The Paper Restriction Enzyme Activity is available for use by other Phage Hunters instructors.

  3. At the end of the semester, students submitted a lab report to describe their efforts to isolate and characterize a novel bacteriophage. I created two intermediate assignments as opportunities for students to receive feedback and revise their work before submitting the final report. First, students were asked to submit a draft of the Introduction and References sections of their report. I developed the assignment and associated grading rubric. Second, I drafted a literature matrix, which guides the process of selecting and synthesizing sources, for students to complete and submit approximately one week before their Introduction was due.
